# COSReady Website Feature Summary

> This document summarizes the product features currently displayed on COSReady’s public website and screenshots shared for interview preparation.  
> It only summarizes what is shown publicly; it does not confirm whether every feature is fully implemented in production.

---

## 1. Product Positioning

COSReady presents itself as a **SaaS platform for beauty, wellness, salon, spa, and medical aesthetic businesses**.

The core positioning is:

> Salon and spa software with an AI front desk.

The website describes the platform as a system that handles:

- bookings;
- client messages;
- reminders;
- follow-ups;
- customer retention;
- daily business operations.

The overall product message is that COSReady is not only a booking calendar. It is positioned as an **all-in-one operating platform** for service-based beauty and wellness businesses.

## 2. Main Product Modules Shown

The website highlights several major modules.

### 2.1 AI Front Desk

The AI Front Desk is one of the most important advertised features.

Displayed capabilities include:

- answering calls;
- answering text messages;
- answering online inquiries;
- working 24/7;
- helping clients book appointments;
- following up with clients;
- supporting phone, text, and web channels.

The value proposition is that a business does not need to rely only on a full-time receptionist, especially after business hours.

### 2.7 Engagement Actions / One QR

The website shows a “One QR” engagement system.

Displayed capabilities include:

- one QR code for multiple customer actions;
- Google review link;
- Instagram follow;
- TikTok follow;
- RedNote link;
- WeChat add/contact;
- store WiFi connection;
- reward points for each action;
- drag-and-drop action ordering;
- per-action reward configuration;
- multilingual customer-facing page;
- branded customer engagement page.

The product flow is shown in three steps:

1. Configure actions that matter to the business.
2. Customers tap the QR page and complete actions.
3. Business measures which actions convert.

This is a strong retention / growth loop feature, especially for physical local businesses.

## 5. Comparison Against Existing Solutions

The website compares COSReady against common existing workflows.

### 5.1 Full-Time Receptionist

Current pain point:

- businesses pay a receptionist to answer calls;
- after-hours calls go to voicemail.

COSReady alternative:

- AI Front Desk handles calls, bookings, and follow-ups 24/7 across phone, text, and web.

---

### 5.2 Calendar-Only Booking Tools

Current pain point:

- booking tools take appointments but do not help with retention or customer return.

COSReady alternative:

- built-in points, rewards, memberships, and marketing campaigns.

---

### 5.3 Disconnected Tools

Current pain point:

- businesses use separate tools for scheduling, CRM, SMS, and reporting.

COSReady alternative:

- one unified platform for AI front desk, scheduling, client communication, and reporting.

---

### 5.4 Manual Reminders

Current pain point:

- staff manually text clients;
- no-shows can hurt weekly revenue.

COSReady alternative:

- automated SMS and WhatsApp reminders, confirmations, and follow-ups.

## 9. Interview-Relevant Interpretation

From an interview perspective, the website suggests that COSReady cares about more than basic full-stack CRUD.

The strongest themes are:

- AI front desk connected to real booking workflows;
- local service business operations;
- customer retention and repeat visits;
- automation of repetitive front-desk work;
- multi-channel communication;
- analytics and business visibility;
- multilingual and multicultural local business use cases.

A candidate can show strong alignment by discussing:

- how AI should safely create or suggest bookings;
- why the backend and database must validate AI actions;
- how to reduce missed calls and no-shows;
- how retention, rewards, and follow-ups connect to revenue;
- how to support multilingual customers;
- how to design AI features that are debuggable and reliable.
